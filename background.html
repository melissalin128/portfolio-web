My Story to Data Science

  My journey to data science was not a straight path. In high school, I was drawn to international relations because I loved public speaking and the analytical thinking I developed through debate. In college, I spent my first 3 semesters exploring different subjects. I challenged myself to take at least one class each semester unrelated to my major, hoping it would spark a new interest or reveal a passion. At the same time, I applied to every internship I could. My goal was simple: either eliminate a path that was not right for me or discover a new opportunity.

  After freshman year, I landed a sales internship and quickly realized I was more interested in the analysis and management side of the team than in sales itself. With the support of my manager, I transitioned to corporate operations, tracking team progress and helping optimize performance. That experience taught me the value of stepping outside my comfort zone, taking initiative, and embracing new challenges.

  Through these experiences, I discovered that I loved math when it was applied to real-world problems and that coding could be a tool to bring insights to life. A friend introduced me to data science, and it immediately felt like the perfect balance of math, coding, and storytelling. Since then, I have been passionate about transforming data into insights that inform decisions and create meaningful impact. Each step along the way, from taking classes outside my comfort zone to pursuing diverse internships, prepared me to approach problems with curiosity, creativity, and persistence.
